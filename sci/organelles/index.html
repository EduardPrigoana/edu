Nucleus

Function

The "control center" of the cell. It contains the cell's genetic material (DNA) and coordinates all cellular activities like growth, metabolism, and cell division.

Cell Type

PlantAnimalEukaryotic

Facts

  • The largest organelle in animal cells.
  • Enclosed by a double membrane called the nuclear envelope.
  • Mature red blood cells lack a nucleus.

Cell Membrane

Function

A selectively permeable barrier that surrounds the cell. It controls the movement of substances in and out, provides protection, and maintains a stable internal environment.

Cell Type

PlantAnimalAll Cells

Facts

  • Described by the fluid mosaic model.
  • Composed of a phospholipid bilayer with embedded proteins.
  • It is flexible and allows cells to change shape.

Mitochondrion

Function

The "powerhouse" of the cell. Generates most of the cell's ATP (energy currency) through cellular respiration.

Cell Type

PlantAnimalEukaryotic

Facts

  • Contains its own small circular DNA (mtDNA).
  • Can self-replicate through binary fission.
  • Believed to have originated from free-living bacteria.

Cytoplasm

Function

A jelly-like substance that fills the cell and surrounds the organelles. It holds organelles in place and is the site of many metabolic reactions, such as glycolysis.

Cell Type

PlantAnimalAll Cells

Facts

  • Composed of about 80% water.
  • The fluid portion is called the cytosol.
  • Cytoplasmic streaming is the movement of cytoplasm within a cell.

Chloroplast

Function

The site of photosynthesis in plant and algal cells. It captures light energy to convert water and carbon dioxide into oxygen and energy-rich organic compounds.

Cell Type

PlantOther (Algae)

Facts

  • Contains the green pigment chlorophyll.
  • Like mitochondria, chloroplasts have their own DNA.
  • Can move within the cell to maximize light absorption.

Vacuole

Function

A membrane-bound sac that plays roles in storage, waste disposal, and maintaining cell shape. In plants, a large central vacuole is crucial for maintaining turgor pressure.

Cell Type

PlantAnimalOther (Fungi)

Facts

  • Can occupy up to 80% of a mature plant cell's volume.
  • Animal cells have smaller, more numerous vacuoles.
  • The membrane surrounding a vacuole is called the tonoplast.

Cell Wall

Function

A rigid outer layer that provides structural support, protection, and filtering. It prevents the cell from bursting due to high internal water pressure.

Cell Type

PlantOther (Fungi, Bacteria)

Facts

  • Made of cellulose in plants, chitin in fungi, and peptidoglycan in bacteria.
  • Animal cells do not have a cell wall.
  • Wood is primarily composed of the cell walls of dead plant cells.
