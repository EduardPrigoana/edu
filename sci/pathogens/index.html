Pathogens

Pathogen Associated Diseases How They Spread Key Characteristics
Bacteria Tuberculosis, Salmonella, Cholera, Tetanus, Gonorrhea Direct contact, contaminated food/water, airborne droplets, vectors. Prokaryotic, single-celled organisms. Reproduce rapidly and can cause disease by producing toxins or damaging host cells. Treatable with antibiotics.
Fungi Athleteâ€™s Foot, Ringworm, Thrush, Aspergillosis Contact with spores (airborne or on surfaces), direct contact with infected skin. Eukaryotic organisms (e.g., yeasts, molds) with cell walls containing chitin. Many infections are opportunistic, affecting those with weakened immune systems. Treated with antifungals.
Protoctists Malaria, Amoebic dysentery, Giardiasis, Leishmaniasis Often through vectors (like mosquitoes, flies), contaminated water or food. A diverse kingdom of single-celled eukaryotic organisms. Often have complex life cycles and may be spread by a vector (e.g., the mosquito for malaria).
Virus Influenza, Common Cold, COVID-19, HIV/AIDS, Measles Airborne droplets, direct contact with bodily fluids, contaminated surfaces (fomites). Acellular obligate parasites; can only reproduce inside living host cells. Consist of genetic material (DNA or RNA) in a protein coat. Vaccines are a key preventative measure.
